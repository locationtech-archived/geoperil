× This enquiry is sent to you to get important input from you for the European ARISTOTLE project. This project has been awarded by EC DG ECHO to a consortium of National (Hydro-) Meteorological Institutes and Geophysical Institutes. Since your institute holds one of the major national responsibilities for weather and hydro related hazards and warnings we herewith ask you to respond to this enquiry before 31st March 2016.

Your prompt response is obviously key to the success of this European project. It might be the case that in your country part of the responsibility is given to regional offices or to a separate institutional bodies (e.g. national or regional hydro institutes). If this is the case we kindly request you to inform us so that we send an invitation for this enquiry to these bodies or institutes for the specific hazard type(s). You should still give your enquiry answers for the hazard for which you have warning responsibilities.

The questionnaire is divided into five sections: Institute, Offices, Contacts, Decision Making Process, and Type of Advice. The questionnaire is already pre-filled with basic information of your Institute, Office and Contact. Please select the Institute, Office or Contact to display and edit this information. Please add information about your institute's regional and local Offices and Contacts as well as information about the Decision Making Process and the Type of Advice by using the blue buttons.

Please provide Contact details for the bodies that are responsible for issuing warnings and advice to civil protection authorities. For each Contact it should be indicated if it is available 24/7 or office hours only. Please fill in at least your institute's 24/7 Contact (if available) and at wish some other Contacts, e.g. for experts and specialists available only during office hours or in exceptional cases. Also please specify the hazard types for which warnings are issued by your institute's national office or regional/federal offices. Please give a brief description with regard to the Decision Making Process and the Type of Advice you provide for the different hazard types. For each hazard type a separate Decision Making Process and Type of Advice could be defined or all could follow the same process and any possibility in between.

All information provided within this questionnaire to the ARISTOTLE consortium will be confidential and will only be shared within the ARISTOTLE consortium unless explicit consent is given. If nevertheless information cannot be shared, respondents are kindly asked to provide a short explanation as to why the information cannot be shared.

In case of questions or problems, please do not hesitate to contact us.
